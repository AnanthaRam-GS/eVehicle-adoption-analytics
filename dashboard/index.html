Model Interpretation

The Random Forest classifier (100 estimators, max depth = 12) achieves 87.2% accuracy on the held-out test set. Charging infrastructure availability is the single strongest predictor (Gini importance 0.182), followed by government incentives (0.156) and perceived cost savings (0.142). The confusion matrix shows the model is slightly better at identifying adopters (recall 0.90) than non-adopters (recall 0.86), suggesting that adoption signals are more distinct. Cross-validation with 5 folds confirms consistent performance.

High Priority

For Manufacturers

Insight: BEVs dominate sales growth, and consumers prioritize range, charging convenience, and total cost of ownership. Price-sensitive segments (Cluster 2) represent the largest untapped market at ~35% of survey respondents.

Recommended Actions:
  • Develop affordable BEV models targeting the $25K–$35K range
  • Invest in solid-state battery R&D to address range concerns
  • Offer flexible financing and subscription models to lower entry barriers
  • Launch targeted marketing for Cluster 2 (price-sensitive) and Cluster 3 (infra-constrained) segments
High Priority

For Governments

Insight: Government incentives are the second-strongest predictor of adoption (importance = 0.156). Countries with comprehensive policy frameworks show 2–3× higher adoption rates than peers.

Recommended Actions:
  • Extend purchase subsidies and tax credits beyond 2030
  • Mandate EV-ready building codes for new construction
  • Invest in public charging infrastructure with focus on rural corridors
  • Set clear ICE phase-out timelines to drive industry planning
Medium Priority

For Investors

Insight: EV stock performance correlates strongly (r = +0.78) with adoption metrics. Battery and charging infrastructure companies show emerging value with market sentiment leading sales data by 6–12 months.

Recommended Actions:
  • Diversify beyond vehicle OEMs into battery supply chain and charging networks
  • Monitor infrastructure deployment rates as a leading indicator of adoption
  • Consider EV software platforms and grid integration companies
  • Track government policy changes as early signals for market shifts
High Priority

For Infrastructure Companies

Insight: Charging infrastructure is the #1 predictor of EV adoption (importance = 0.182). Current vehicle-to-charger ratio of 6.2:1 is improving but gaps remain in suburban and rural corridors.

Recommended Actions:
  • Prioritize highway corridor fast-charging (150kW+) networks
  • Partner with retail and hospitality chains for destination charging
  • Invest in smart grid integration and V2G (Vehicle-to-Grid) capabilities
  • Target underserved suburban and rural areas for maximum adoption impact

Strategic Outlook

The transition to electric mobility is at a critical inflection point. Our analysis across 7 datasets and 325 survey respondents shows that the top 3 adoption drivers — infrastructure availability, government incentives, and cost savings — are all addressable through coordinated stakeholder action. Markets that align policy, infrastructure, and product strategies are poised to reach mass adoption (>30% penetration) within the next 5 years.

About This Project

Project overview, methodology, data sources, and team

Project Description

This capstone project analyzes global electric vehicle (EV) adoption, market performance, charging infrastructure readiness, and financial market signals. It combines secondary datasets from reliable public sources with primary survey data to derive actionable business insights.

Research title: "EV Market Dynamics: Analyzing Consumer Trends and Financial Signals"

Datasets Used

  • IEA Global EV Data Explorer
  • U.S. Electric Vehicle Population Data (data.gov)
  • Open Charge Map — Charging Infrastructure
  • Opendatabay — Consumer EV Adoption Dataset
  • Global EV Sales Dataset (2010–2024)
  • Primary Survey (Google Forms, 325 responses)

Methodologies

  • K-Means Clustering — Consumer segmentation into 4 distinct clusters
  • Random Forest Classification — Identifying key drivers of EV adoption
  • Time-Series Analysis — Forecasting EV market growth trends
  • Correlation & Regression — EV sales vs. financial market signals
  • Bootstrap Sampling — Augmenting survey data for statistical robustness
